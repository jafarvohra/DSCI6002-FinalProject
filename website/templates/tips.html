Tips to Mitigate the Risk of Heart Attack Based on Important Model Features




Eat a Healthy Diet:

- Include a variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins in your diet.
- Limit saturated and trans fats, cholesterol, salt, and added sugars.
- Choose heart-healthy fats such as those found in olive oil, avocados, and nuts.

Maintain a Healthy BMI:

- Strive for a healthy body mass index on your age, height, and weight.
- Regular physical activity and a balanced diet can help with weight management.

Exercise Regularly:

- Engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week.
- Include strength training exercises at least two days per week.

Quit Smoking:

- If you smoke, quit through the use of alternative methods.
- Seek support from healthcare professionals, counseling, or smoking cessation programs.

Limit Alcohol Intake:

- If you choose to drink alcohol, do so in moderation.
- If dependent on alcohol, seek support from healthcare professionals or AA programs.

Manage Stress:

- Practice stress-reducing techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, yoga, or other relaxation methods.
- Engage in activities that bring joy and relaxation.

Get Quality Sleep:

- Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night.
- Establish a regular sleep schedule and create a comfortable sleep environment.
